ABOUT ME

    Soccer and curiosity, have been my two biggest motivators in life. I've always loved disecting things. Whether it be a mouse in highschool biology to taking apart my calculator, the way things work have always sparked my interest. I love getting to see the way something runs or works. "Why does that tv's picture look the way that it does?" and "How does my body know when to sweat?". On the other hand, soccer has also had a huge influence on my life. Everything about soccer, amazes me. I love how you can never be the best at soccer because there's alway room for improvement, somewhere. Knowing that I can always do better and get better and be better, is what will always keep me coming back to this sport. Growing up, I've learned a lot from this sport. Not only how to work hard, but also how to be a team player, and work with all kinds of people. I've had the chance to play with guys from around the world and it's taught me that everyone is unique in their own way. That means that the way I conduct myself has to be unique to that person.

    With these two interests in mind, I decided that I wanted to be a doctor. Of course, after my professional soccer career was finished. However, my dream of getting to that level of soccer was cut short with a career ending injury. I ended up studying Biology with a minor in Chem to combat my shortcomings. In my studies, I realized that even though I love learning about the deepest roots of life itself, I was not able to continue down this path. That's when I came acrosse programming, and I've been hooked ever since.

    Software programming allows me to use my strengths to their fullest capacities. While, at the same time, I am able to strengthen my weaknesses. I am able to see how websites or any type of applications are built. I also get to create my own projects, by turning my ideas into a tangible product. I am able to do all this while also getting the opportunity to work with other people and teams. Software Programming may not have been my first career choice, but it will be my last.
